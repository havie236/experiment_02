In this task, you will be asked to count specific items. You may be asked to:

  • Count the number of ones (1) in a table containing 1s and 0s
  • Count the number of letters “m” in a table containing m and w
  • Count the number of UP-pointing triangles (▲) in a table containing UP (▲) and DOWN (▼) triangles

You will receive a monetary reward for each correct count. The amount earned per correct answer decreases slightly after each correct answer.

For example:

  • 1st correct answer: 1,107 VND

This means that each additional correct answer earns a bit less than the previous one

There are 03 sessions, each lasting up to 15 minutes. Your total earnings will be the sum of your earnings across all three sessions.

You may stop a session at any time and proceed to the next session. You are free to do other non-task activities on your laptop (e.g., browsing, social media). However, you must complete the task independently. The use of AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT), automated counting tools, or any external help to assist with counting or solving the task is strictly prohibited.

Debriefing Statement

Thank you for participating in this study.

The purpose of this experiment is to understand how simply seeing information about a peer’s earnings influences your effort in a task. Will you work harder to catch up with them? Similar to how people react when exposed to salary information on social media?

During the experiment, you may have seen information about the average number of tasks completed by previous participants, or you may not have seen any such information. This information was intentionally varied across different parts of the experiment so that we could study how different reference points (higher, lower, or no benchmark) affect work behavior.

It is important to note that:

  • The benchmark information did not affect your payment rate.
  • There is no expected, correct, or "ideal" level of performance in this study.
  • The benchmark numbers are used only for research purposes and are not a judgment of your ability or effort.
  • You were free to stop working at any time, and your decisions about how long to continue working are a key part of what we are studying.

All data collected in this study are anonymous and will be analyzed only in aggregate. Your participation will not affect your academic standing in any way.

If you experienced any frustration or fatigue during the task, this is completely normal. If you have any concerns or questions about the study, you may contact the researcher or the faculty advisor using the information provided in the consent form.

Thank you again for your time and contribution to this research.
